About

Who I am

Over two decades of working across franchises, startups, corporates, hospitality, construction, and conscious projects around the world.

In every one of those environments, the same pattern kept showing up: businesses that started with a genuine purpose would gradually lose touch with the people they were built to serve. The focus would drift from customers and teams to margins and spreadsheets. Quality would quietly decline.

The root cause was almost always the same: broken systems putting unnecessary pressure on good people. Manual processes, disconnected tools, duplicated work, missed details. Teams drowning in admin instead of doing what they were hired to do. The technology to fix this existed, but it was not reaching the businesses that needed it most.

Grand Vision Perspective was founded to change that. I look at what appears to be operational chaos and find the clear path to simplicity. Then I build it. Finance systems, CRM and revenue pipelines, AI agents, internal tools, infrastructure, the whole stack. Always end to end, always practical, always measured by the hours saved and the problems eliminated.

The last several years have concentrated that work in a particularly demanding arena: running product, operations, and systems at the intersection of hardware and software. Leading development of a commercial wellness technology product from prototype through international manufacturing. Coordinating supply chains across Indonesia, the Philippines, and China. Building the finance, CRM, and operations systems that keep multiple companies running day to day. Shipping my own personal AI assistant infrastructure. Rehabilitating broken automation platforms that were causing outages.

Based in Bali, working across Indonesia, Asia, and internationally.

How I work

Four phases, one operator.

Most consultancies stop at the plan. Most builders arrive without one. I do both, in sequence, so the work actually lands in the business and keeps running after handover.

01

Audit and Discovery

I walk into your operations and come out with a clear map. What is working, what is wasting money, what is waiting to break, what should exist that does not. Delivered as a prioritised roadmap you can act on, with or without me.

02

Design and Architecture

Turning the audit into a concrete systems plan. Tools, workflows, integrations, data model, sequencing, and realistic timelines. Written in language both the business and engineering sides can act on.

03

Build and Ship

Solo delivery of the system, end to end. I write the code, connect the services, configure the platforms, document the handover, and train your team. No throwing work over a wall.

04

Operate and Iterate

Optional ongoing engagement to run, monitor, extend, and evolve the system as the business grows. This is where most consultancies stop. I stay.

Principles

How I actually work.

  • Discovery starts with your workflow, not a framework.I watch how work actually moves in your business and find where the tax is being paid. The system gets designed around reality.
  • Every recommendation is justified by the cost of the problem it replaces.No nice-to-haves. If it does not pay for itself, it does not ship.
  • Working systems, not plans.I deliver things that run, not deliverables that sit in a folder.
  • No lock-in.Documentation and handover are part of the work. Your team should be able to run and extend the system without me.
  • Security and reliability are baked in.Secrets in vaults, not spreadsheets. Audit trails by default. Backups tested, not assumed.
  • Scope honesty.If something is more expensive or harder than it looks, I say so before the contract. If something is easier, the same.
