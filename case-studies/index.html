Case Study 01

End-to-End Customer Journey Automation

A product company with a complex sales-to-delivery lifecycle

Challenge

Every step of the customer journey was manual. Leads came in from the website and sat in inboxes. Qualification was inconsistent. Follow-ups were missed. Once a sale was made, production tracking lived in spreadsheets, logistics was coordinated by phone, and clients had no visibility on their order. The team was stretched thin doing work that should have been automatic.

Solution

We designed and implemented a complete lifecycle system. Leads are automatically captured, profiled, scored, and routed. High-potential prospects are flagged for personal outreach while others receive tailored content based on how they move through the pipeline. Post-sale, the system tracks production, triggers payment reminders, manages logistics, and keeps clients informed at every stage.

Results

Manual touchpoints reduced by 80%. Customer response times dropped from days to hours. The sales team doubled their outreach capacity without adding a single person. Staff retention improved because people had space to focus on craft instead of chasing admin.

80%Fewer manual touchpoints
2×Sales team capacity
Case Study 02

Multi-Site Hospitality Launch Programme

A gastropub group expanding across multiple locations

Challenge

The group needed to open a new venue every three months across different locations in the country. Each site had its own local challenges, regulations, suppliers, and staffing requirements. Without standardised systems, every launch was starting from scratch. Teams were burning out, timelines were slipping, and quality was inconsistent.

Solution

We created a repeatable operational playbook. Each three-month cycle covered recruitment, team training, systems implementation, local marketing, the launch honeymoon period, and structured handover. Every process was documented, templated, and refined with each successive site. By the third launch, the playbook was running like clockwork.

Results

Seven venues launched on schedule across 21 months. Each launch was faster and smoother than the last. Customer experience was consistent across every location because the systems were consistent. Staff turnover dropped with each successive launch as the onboarding process matured.

7Venues launched on schedule
21Months, end to end
Case Study 03

Operational Automation at Scale

A growing organisation with disconnected systems across departments

Challenge

The team was spending more time on admin than on the work that actually mattered. CRM updates were manual. Financial reporting required hours of spreadsheet compilation. Marketing, sales, and operations each used different tools that did not talk to each other. Information lived in silos, and the people who needed it most could not find it.

Solution

We mapped every manual process, identified the bottlenecks, and designed an integrated automation layer connecting CRM, email, accounting, and project management into one cohesive system. Automated workflows now handle lead routing, follow-ups, invoicing, reporting, and internal notifications. Real-time dashboards replaced weekly manual reports.

Results

Over 15 hours reclaimed every week across the team. Data entry errors virtually disappeared. Reports that used to take half a day now generate themselves. The sales team started closing faster because they could see their entire pipeline in real time. Customer follow-up became instant instead of sporadic.

15+Hours reclaimed weekly
0Manual data entry errors
Case Study 04

Multi-Entity Financial Transformation

A group of businesses with no unified financial visibility

Challenge

Three separate businesses, each with its own accounting mess. No reconciliation process. No profit and loss visibility. Bank transactions piling up unprocessed. The leadership team was making decisions in the dark, unable to see the financial health of any entity, let alone the group as a whole.

Solution

We designed and built a unified financial management system across all three entities. Automated ledger processing, transaction categorisation, bank reconciliation, and real-time P&L dashboards. Each entity maintains its own books while leadership gets a consolidated group view at the touch of a button.

Results

Full financial visibility across all three entities for the first time. Monthly close went from days of manual work to hours. The finance team shifted from data entry to strategic analysis. Decision-making improved across the board because leadership could finally see the numbers in real time.

3Entities unified
90%Faster monthly close
